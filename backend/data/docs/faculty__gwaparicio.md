## About Me

German W Aparicio Jr is 0-60 Accelerator Co-Founder, Program Manager of Early Innovation and Partner Development at Trimble Ventures and Faculty member at Cal Poly Pomona

As Program Manager of Early Innovation and Partner Development at Trimble Ventures German is focused on advancing early-stage innovation and collaboration, leading outreach efforts and expanding the Trimble Ventures innovation portfolio through the identification of promising early-stage companies. His responsibilities include nurturing partnerships within the global education, startup, and platform ecosystem at Trimble in an attempt to elevate the value of our portfolio companies. His goal is to help in cultivating an innovation-centric culture within our company and help drive positive change within our industries.

German received a Bachelor of Architecture from Cal Poly Pomona where he was awarded a thesis design award by the department and a Master of Science in Architectural Studies (SMArchS) Design and Computation from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT). He has taught architectural design at MIT, California College of Arts (CCA) in San Francisco, University of California, Berkeley, University of California, Los Angeles and Cal Poly Pomona.
