# Academic Standing

## What is Academic Standing?

Academic Standing is defined by your cumulative, Cal Poly Pomona and/or major grade point averages (GPAs). A GPA is a calculated average of the letter grades you earn. If you want to learn more about the letter grades CPP uses, visit the [University Catalog](https://catalog.cpp.edu/) (navigate to the Academic Policies section and click on Grading System).

You can check your GPAs on your unofficial transcript or Degree Progress Report. Both are located in your [BroncoDirect Student Center](https://my.cpp.edu/).

Your Academic Standing is updated each term after you receive your final grades.

Read the information below to learn about the different academic standings at Cal Poly Pomona that reflect your current progress and may impact your future enrollment.

## More About Academic Standing for Undergraduates

### Good Standing

#### What It Means

Your Cumulative and CPP GPA are 2.0 or above.

### Academic Warning (Formerly At-Risk)

#### What It Means

You have a CPP or Major GPA between a 2.0 and a 2.2.

#### What Will Happen

The Academic Warning hold is placed on your account, and the hold will impact your future registration.

#### Next Steps

In order to remove the hold, you must complete a Canvas based module. Once completed, the hold will be removed automatically within 48 hours.

### Academic Notice (Formerly Academic Probation)

#### What It Means

You have a Cumulative or CPP GPA below 2.0, but it is above the level for Academic Notice for Contract.

The first time you fall into Academic Notice for Contract standing (see requirements below), you will be placed on Academic Notice.

#### What Will Happen

The Academic Notice hold is placed on your account and the hold will impact your future registration.

#### Next Steps

In order to remove the hold, you must complete three steps:

1. Complete a Canvas-based module.
2. Complete the Academic Notice Worksheet linked at the end of the Canvas module.
3. Meet with your [Retention and Graduation Specialist](../../retention-graduation-specialists.shtml). After this meeting, the hold will be removed.

### Academic Notice with Contract (Formerly Academic Probation with Contract)

#### What It Means

You have a Cumulative or CPP GPA below the following levels based on class standing:

Table of Academic Notice with Contract GPAs by Class Standing

| Class Standing | Semester Units Completed | Grade Point Average |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Freshman | Less than 30 | <1.5 |
| Sophomore | 30-59 | <1.7 |
| Junior | 60-89 | <1.85 |
| Senior | 90 or more | <1.95 |

#### What Will Happen

The Academic Notice with Contract hold is placed on your account and the hold will impact your future registration. You should [use our Academic Notice with Contract tool](disqualification.shtml) to understand the specific impact for future terms.

#### Next Steps

In order to remove the hold, you must complete three steps:

1. Complete a Canvas-based module.
2. Complete an Academic Notice with Contract worksheet and contract linked at the end of the Canvas module.
3. Finally, meet with your [Retention and Graduation Specialist](../../retention-graduation-specialists.shtml). After this meeting, the hold will be removed.

### Administrative Academic Notice (Formerly Administrative Probation)

You can be placed on Administrative Academic Notice if you are not making progress in your current major. You will receive a Student Success Milestone hold (SSM).  This hold does not prevent registration. The hold encourages you to schedule a meeting with your assigned advisor as soon as possible. In that meeting, you and your advisor will complete an Administrative Academic Notice agreement.

An example of Administrative Academic Notice may include if you have not been taking courses in your current major for at least one term, which may include courses for a major you have not declared.

If you do not meet the terms of the Administrative Academic Notice agreement, you may be discontinued from the university or have a hold placed that will prevent registration for future terms.

Please visit the University Catalog for details on Administrative Academic Notice.

### Disqualification

**What It Means**

Undergraduate students can fall into disqualification in two ways:

1. If you do not meet the term GPA of 2.0 or higher at the end of any term following your first Academic Notice with Contract status (for as long as you remain on Academic Notice with Contract status).
2. If, after your first Academic Notice with Contract status, you successfully raise your CPP and Overall GPA above the Academic Notice with Contract threshold, but then your GPA falls below the threshold in a subsequent term, you will be automatically disqualified from CPP.

**What Will Happen**

If you are academically disqualified, you will be discontinued from the university in the next term. This means that if you are enrolled in classes in the next term, you will be dropped from those classes. It also means that you will need to reapply to the institution if you want to enroll in the future as a matriculated student.

**Next Steps**

Students who are interested in continuing their education after academic disqualification have a few options.

If you are interested in returning, visit the [Returning Student website](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/returning-students/index.shtml). Complete the form and a member of the Bronco Advising Center team will reach out to you to discuss options for readmissions. Options may include [repeating courses through Open University](https://www.cpp.edu/cpge/open-university/registration/index.shtml) or submitting a [Retroactive Withdrawal petition](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/oss/academic-advising/university-policy-information/retroactive-withdrawal.shtml), among others. Only after your CPP GPA has improved will you be eligible to reapply; however, improving your GPA above a 2.0 does not guarantee that you will be readmitted.

If returning to CPP is not an option, the Bronco Advising Center team member will also discuss options for applying to other institutions.

## More About Academic Standing for Graduates

### Good Standing

#### What It Means

Your Cumulative and CPP GPA are 3.0 or above.

### Academic Notice (Formerly Probation)

#### What It Means

You have a CPP or Major GPA below a 3.0, but it's above the level for Academic Notice with Contract.

#### What Will Happen

The Academic Notice hold is placed on your account and the hold will impact your future registration.

#### Next Steps

In order to remove the hold, you must complete two steps:

1. First, meet with your graduate advisor.
2. After meeting with your graduate advisor, you must have your hold removed by your graduate coordinator, department chair, or designee.

### Academic Notice with Contract (Formerly Probation with Contract)

#### What It Means

You have a Cumulative or CPP GPA below the following levels based on units completed:

Table of Academic Notice With Contract GPAs by Units Completed

| Semester Units Completed | Grade Point Average |
| --- | --- |
| 8 | <2.3 |
| 16 | <2.7 |
| 24 | <2.9 |

#### What Will Happen

The Academic Notice with Contract hold is placed on your account and the hold will impact your future registration.

#### Next Steps

In order to remove the hold, you must complete these steps:

1. Familiarize yourself with university and college academic policies and procedures. Additional information regarding academic standing may be obtained from the [University Catalog](https://catalog.cpp.edu/). For Catalog review, please scroll to the subheading in Academic Policies titled “Academic Standing – Postbaccalaureate Students.”
2. Complete the Academic Notice with Contract Worksheet and Academic Notice with Contract Agreement. Contact your graduate coordinator if you need assistance with filling out these forms.
3. Meet with your graduate coordinator or department chair immediately for academic advising to determine how to regain good standing. The Academic Notice with Contract worksheet and Agreement Form must be approved by your graduate coordinator.
   After meeting with your graduate coordinator, you must have the hold removed by your graduate coordinator, department chair, or designee.
