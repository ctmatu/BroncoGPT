# US Bank Corporate Travel Card Program

![](../img/credit card.jpg)

The US Bank Corporate Travel Card program offers a timely and reliable way to pay for travel and is available to employees who must travel on behalf of the University. It eliminates the need to personally pay for travel related expenses and then seek reimbursement. The Travel Card charges are automatically recorded to the department chartfield that is established at the time of card setup. If the traveler wants to allocate a charge to a different chartfield, that option is available through the US Bank Access Online website.

University travelers have the option of using one of the following Travel Cards:

1. The Individual Travel Credit Card (frequent travelers, ≥ 3 times/year)

-OR-

2. The Departmental Travel Credit Card (infrequent travelers, < 3 times/year)

The Individual Travel Card can be used for all expenses incurred for University business related travel, both pre-paid travel expenses and those incurred during travel; while the Departmental Travel Card can be used to prepay expenses for faculty and staff travelers on official University business, such as registration fees, airfare and lodging. Use of the Travel Card program is optional for faculty and staff traveling on official University business. Travelers can utilize their personal credit cards to pay for travel expenses incurred on University business travel, with subsequent reimbursement requested through the submission of an expense report in Concur.

The Travel Card program can only be used for State funded travel transactions; therefore, CPP Foundation, Philanthropic Foundation and ASI travel purchases cannot be made with these cards.  University Accounting Services, and Accounts Payable, will review and monitor all cardholder purchases to ensure they are in alignment with the Travel Card procedures.

## Financial Responsibility

The University is responsible for the payment of transactions for the Travel Card program. Transactions placed on the Individual or Departmental Travel Cards will not affect the cardholder’s personal credit history. Cardholder statements will be paid in full, including disputed items, to avoid late fees and penalties. For disputed charges, it is the cardholder’s responsibility to secure a reversal of charges (credit) from the vendor and ensure the account is credited within 10-days from the date of dispute.

## Authorized Cardholders

The Corporate Individual Travel Card will be issued to employees who travel frequently (more than 3 times a year) on university business. Eligible employees include full-time staff or faculty with a minimum appointment of 6 months or greater. Individual Travel Cards are issued in the name of the individual traveler requesting the card and are intended solely for their use for university business travel.

The Corporate Departmental Travel Card will be issued only to staff who are responsible for arranging and coordinating travel for their department. Eligible employees include full-time staff with a minimum appointment of 6 months or greater. Departmental Travel Cards will not be issued to individuals for solely their use. However, with the appropriate preapproval of travel, a cardholder may make arrangements using their Departmental Travel Card when they themselves are the traveler.

## Billing Cycle & Reconciliation Due Date

The billing cycle ends on the 15th of each month. Exception: when the 15th falls on a weekend or holiday, the billing cycle will end on the following business day.

Reconciliation must be submitted electronically to travel@cpp.edu and received in University Accounting Services no later than 5:00 p.m. on the 1st of the following month. Exception: when the 1st falls on a weekend or holiday, the due date will be the end of the following business day.
