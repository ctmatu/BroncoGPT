# Cold War Discussion

## About the Discussion

California's Secrets of the Cold War is an event that discussed how secret keeping shaped families and culture during the Cold War.  In addition, participants had a chance to explore how the Cold War influenced California's ethnic diversity. These discussions were based on readings from Karen Piper's *A Girl's Guide to Missiles: Growing Up in America's Secret Desert,*Mihir Pandya’s article *“The Vanishing Act: Stealth Airplanes and Cold War Southern California,”*and Thi Bui's *The Best We Could Do*.  Dylan Bennett and Gabriel Castrillon served as discussion leaders for this dialogue series and they facilitated events at Cal Poly Pomona and the Richard Nixon Presidential Library and Museum.
