# Change of Major Guide

## Step 4: Apply to Change Your Major

Once you’ve completed any requirements you need to fulfill, attended a change of major meeting (if applicable) and you’ve talked to an advisor in the [department](../a-z.shtml#dept), you need to let the [Registrar’s Office](https://www.cpp.edu/~registrar/) know that you are officially changing your [major](../a-z.shtml#major). You do this by [filling out the Change of Major form](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/forms/forms.shtml), available as an e-form on their website. Here are some things to keep in mind:

* **For some majors, you can submit the change of major form at any time.**
  + For others, you may have a specific deadline to officially change your major that year. The tool includes any special instructions about timing.
* **The form must be approved.**
  + The department chair in the major checks that you’ve fulfilled the requirements and approves the form before it goes to the Registrar’s Office.
* **It takes some time to get approved, and have it reflected on your record.**
  + If you have questions about the status of your form, you can follow up with your [academic department](../a-z.shtml#dept) for updates.
* **When you change your major, you change your curriculum year.**
  + Your [curriculum year](../a-z.shtml#curry) is the year at the top of the [curriculum sheet](../a-z.shtml#currs) you received when you attended [Orientation](../a-z.shtml#ori). For example, if you joined the university in 2024, you received a copy of the 2024-25 curriculum sheet for your major. If you change your major, you’ll follow your new major’s curriculum sheet for the academic year you switched. If you are a student who started on quarters, make sure that you talk to your academic advisor about how a change of major will affect your [General Education](../a-z.shtml#ge) requirements and progress.
* **You will receive email communication about the form’s processing.** Keep tabs on your CPP email for any emails from polydoc@cpp.edu.
* **If your change of major form is denied, you should talk to the department chair.** They make the final decision.
