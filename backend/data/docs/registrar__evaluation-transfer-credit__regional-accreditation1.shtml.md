# Regional Accreditation

## Regional Accrediting Agencies

Cal Poly Pomona only awards credit from regionally accredited institutions. An unlimited number of units may be transferred from regionally accredited four-year universities; however, students must complete the minimum residency requirements. A maximum of 70 semester units or 105 quarter units transferred from community colleges may be applied toward the cumulative unit requirement for a bachelor’s degree.

**Regional accrediting agencies:**

• [Middle States Commission on Higher Education (MSA)](https://www.msche.org/)
• [New England Association of Schools and Colleges, Commission on Institutions of Higher Education (NEASC-CIHE)](https://www.neche.org/)
• [Northwest Commission of Colleges and Universities (NWCCU)](https://nwccu.org/)
• [North Central Association of Colleges and Schools, The Higher Learning Commission (NCA-HLC)](https://www.hlcommission.org/)
• [Southern Association of Colleges and Schools Commission on Colleges (SACS-COC)](https://www.sacscoc.org/)
• [Western Association of Schools and Colleges, Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (WASC-ACCJC)](https://accjc.org/)
• [WASC Senior College and University Commission (WSCUC)](https://www.wscuc.org/)

Students who complete coursework at non-regionally accredited institutions may request their major department to review coursework after successfully completing 20-semester units at Cal Poly Pomona. Upon the recommendation of the major department, credit may be awarded.

To find the accrediting agency for your institution, go to the [DAPIP](https://ope.ed.gov/dapip/#/home) Website.
