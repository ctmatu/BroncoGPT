# Health & Wellness Resources

International students made huge financial, physical, and mental commitments to study in the U.S.  Many international students are under tremendous personal and family pressure to earn degrees in the U.S., however, one thing that is often overlooked is how to maintain a healthy state of mind while pursuing their goals and dealing with pressure.

CPP provides a number of support services to the campus community. You can learn about how to use the services below.

## Student Health and Wellness Services

They provide affordable, accessible and high-quality health care. Most services are covered by the mandatory student health fee already included in tuition payments.

[Learn more about Student Health and Wellness Services](https://www.cpp.edu/health/student-health-services.shtml)

## Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS)

They provides services such as workshops, group or individual therapy, wellbeing coaching, crisis intervention, and support to access on- and off-campus resources. Currently enrolled students are able to utilize services at no additional fee.

[Learn More about Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS)](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/index.shtml)

## Care Center

The Care Center is the first stop for wellbeing, basic needs, mental health support and more.

[Learn More about Care Center](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/)

## Campus Recreation

They provide recreational opportunities that inspire and cultivate healthy lifestyles, continuous personal growth, and an inclusive community.

[Learn More about Campus Recreation](https://asi.cpp.edu/campusrec/)

## YOU@CPP

Find tips and tools for everything from your mental and physical health, to friendships and finding balance. It’s all here at Cal Poly Pomona.

[Learn more about YOU@CPP](https://you.cpp.edu/)

[## Additional Resources

Click here for additional resources and services.](https://www.cpp.edu/campus-life/index.shtml)

## Crisis Help

For immediate crisis assistance, call the University Police Department emergency number at (909) 869-3070 or dial 911.

For medical, counseling or sexual assault survivor assistance, call Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) at 909-869-3220. If calling after hours, follow the recorded instructions to be connected with an on-call provider.
