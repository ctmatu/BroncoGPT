### Narcan and Fentanyl Test Strips Distribution

This life-saving medication is now available to the CPP community for free! Narcan (naloxone) is a medication used to reverse opioid overdose. Examples of opioids include heroin, oxycodone (OxyContin/Percocet), Fentanyl, Morphine, Codeine, and Hydrocodone (Vicodin). To obtain free Narcan and Fentanyl test strips complete the Narcan Distribution Program Training.](#modal-1 "Narcan Training Form")

### Peer Health Educator Session (Wellness Card)

A brief 30-minute session with a Peer Health Educator (PHE) designed to help increase a students’ knowledge of health, healthy behaviors or harm reduction. A PHE will provide a student with an assessment and education on one of the following topics: stress, time management, nutrition, alcohol, or physical activity. Completing a wellness card may be required by some professors or offered as extra credit.](#modal-2 "Wellness Cards")

### Wellness Cards

×

The Wellness Card program gives students the opportunity to assess their health and wellness, learn how to maximize health, and become familiar with health and wellness services at Cal Poly Pomona. Our goal is to help you stay well and make informed choices.

**How Does it Work?**

1. **Part 1:** Complete 1 online assessment
   * [Nutrition](documents/nutrition-assessment-2021.pdf)
   * [Physical Activity](documents/physical-activity-assessment-planner.pdf)
   * [Sleep](documents/sleep-assessment-awareness.pdf)
   * [Stress](documents/assess-your-stress-interactive.pdf)
   * [Time Management](documents/time-management-assessment.pdf)
2. **Part 2:** Schedule and complete a 1:1 [wellness card coaching session](https://calendly.com/wellnessctr-1/30min?month=2023-02) with a Peer Health Educator.
3. A certificate of completion will be provided after completing Part 1 and 2

##### **For Faculty**

Looking for an out-of-class activity that is educational and beneficial for your class? The Wellness Card has been well-received by many faculty on campus. Research shows that healthier students perform better academically\*.

###### *\*Sources:*

* University of Minnesota. "Grades In College Directly Linked To Health-Related Behaviors." ScienceDaily 22 October 2008.
* M. Scott Deberard, Glen I. Spielmans, Deana L. Julka; Predictors of Academic Achievement and Retention among College Freshmen: A Longitudinal Study; College Student Journal, Vol. 38, 2004
