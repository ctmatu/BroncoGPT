# Student Resources

## Resources for You

[### CPP Registrar

This is where you can access important forms through the Registar’s Office (registration forms, withdrawal forms, graduation and diploma forms, and VA forms).](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/forms/forms.shtml "opens a new window")

[### Student Success Central

This site contains links to the overall CPP Advising Center.](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/index.shtml "opens a new window")

[### The Care Center

This is a comprehensive campus-wide initiative that serves students experiencing housing instability, food insecurity, and other emergency/crisis situations.](https://www.cpp.edu/broncoscarecenter/ "opens a new window")

[### CLASS (our College)

This is the website for the College of Letters, Arts and Social Sciences (the College to which History belongs).](https://www.cpp.edu/class/index.shtml "opens a new window")

[### IT&T (Technology)

This site contains all sorts of helpful tech-related links for students: download (for free) software like Microsoft Office, and access the virtual software lab; set up your internet connection on campus; register for 2-step authentication, etc.](https://www.cpp.edu/it/students/ "opens a new window")

[### Learning Resource Center

This is the University’s comprehensive tutoring service -- you can schedule appointments to meet with tutors for writing, math, etc.](https://www.cpp.edu/lrc/)

[### Student Health Services

Click here for Student Health Services, where you can make appointments, check your records, etc.](https://www.cpp.edu/health/index.shtml "opens a new window")

[### Career Center

The Career Center helps you prepare for careers after CPP. Check out their workshops on applying for jobs and internships, writing your resumé, etc.!](https://careercenter.cpp.edu/?cppshorturl=career "opens a new window")

[### CPP Library

This is the overall link to the Cal Poly Pomona Library. History students should also check out the overall guides compiled by the Library Subject Librarians (click on “History” for the expanded list of research guides) and should also consult the History Department’s own extensive guide to finding sources).](https://www.cpp.edu/library/index.shtml "opens a new window")
