# Faculty Pre-Retirement and Early Retirement Teaching Options

---

**Pre-Retirement Reduction in Time Base (PRTB),** outlined in [Article 30 (pdf)](https://www2.calstate.edu/csu-system/faculty-staff/labor-and-employee-relations/Documents/unit3-cfa/article30.pdf) of the CBA allows eligible faculty to work less than full-time for up to five years prior to retirement while continuing to accrue full service credit toward retirement.

Deadline to apply for PRTB participation: **FEBRUARY 16, 2026**

* [PRTB Request Form (pdf)](../documents/prtb_form-3.pdf "PRTB Request Form")
* [FERP vs PRTB (pdf)](../documents/ferp-vs-prtb-comparison.pdf)

**The Faculty Early Retirement Program (FERP),** outlined in [Article 29 (pdf)](https://www2.calstate.edu/csu-system/faculty-staff/labor-and-employee-relations/Documents/unit3-cfa/article29.pdf "Article 29") of the CBA allows tenured faculty eligible for CalPERS retirement to retire and continue teaching for a maximum of five consecutive years at a time base of fifty percent (50%) of the employee's regular time base in the year preceding retirement.

Deadline to apply for FERP participation: **FEBRUARY 16, 2026**

* [FERP Request Form (pdf)](../documents/ferp-request-form-april-2020.pdf) and [Retired Annuitant Certification (pdf)](../documents/retiredannuitantcertification2.pdf)

## Emeritus Faculty Status

Emeritus status is given upon retirement to faculty with a minimum of 10 years of university service. Departments are asked to send their recommendations to the Academic Senate Office (98-P2-8) before the April deadline.

* [Emeritus Privileges for Faculty (pdf)](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty-affairs/documents/Faculty_Privileges_2010.pdf)
* [Policy for Granting Emeritus Status to Faculty (pdf)](https://www.cpp.edu/academic-manual/1300-1399-academic-personnel-policies/1386-1392/policy_1391_granting_emeritus_status_to_faculty.pdf)

## Post-Retirement Engagement

[The California State University Emeritus and Retired Faculty & Staff Association (CSU-ERFSA)](https://www.csuerfsa.org/)

CSU-ERFSA is a statewide organization open to all CSU system retired faculty and staff members. Emeritus status is not required. CSU-ERFSA publishes a [newsletter](https://www.csuerfsa.org/index.php) and also has a [grant awards program](https://www.csuerfsa.org/index.php) for retired faculty members who are continuing their research and scholarly activities.  CSU-ERFSA membership is required to be eligible to compete for funding for this program.

[Osher Lifelong Learning Institutes (OLLIs)](https://sps.northwestern.edu/oshernrc/about/osher-lifelong-learning-institute-network.php)

The 124 Osher Lifelong Learning Institutes (OLLIs) span all 50 states and the District of Columbia. Each is a part of a college or university and is funded in part by The Bernard Osher Foundation.   [Find an OSHER Institute near you.](https://sps.northwestern.edu/oshernrc/find-an-osher-near-you.php)
