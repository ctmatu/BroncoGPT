# How to Support CCEP

The California Center for Ethics and Policy brings together local and national leaders in ethics and policy to think critically and creatively with Cal Poly Pomona students and faculty about the world's most pressing challenges – including [climate change](../annual-ccep/2021-22.shtml "climate change"), [housing insecurity](../annual-ccep/2020-21.shtml "housing insecurity"), [migration](../annual-ccep/2022-23.shtml "migration"), and artificial intelligence. We support our exceptional CPP [Student](../fellows/student-fellows.shtml "Student Fellows") and [Faculty Fellows](../fellows/faculty-fellows.shtml "Faculty Fellows") as they engage the local community, pursue cutting-edge research, and share what they learn with the wider public. We do this through a series of interdisciplinary panels featuring a mix of policymakers, activists, and scholars; through our student-driven [CCEP podcasts](../podcast.shtml); and through impactful community initiatives, such as the [Election Fairs](https://thepolypost.com/news/2020/09/21/cpp-prepares-for-2020-election/ "Election Fairs") we host to raise civic awareness and voter turnout. We seek out opportunities to promote equity wherever we can, such as through [Project Mailbox](https://thepolypost.com/news/2021/08/31/project-mailbox-expected-to-arrive-later-this-semester/ "Project Mailbox"), an initiative we spearheaded to enable housing-insecure students to receive their mail on campus.

Your contributions are essential to advancing our mission, supporting our work at CPP and surrounding communities, and to promoting a most just California and a more ethical world.
