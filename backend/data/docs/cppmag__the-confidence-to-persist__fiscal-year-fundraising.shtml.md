# Fiscal Year Fundraising Update

## Who Is Donating

Together **6,370** donors gave **11,328** gifts to programs across campus.

* 55% Alumni
* 15% Friends
* 10% Students
* 9% Organizations
* 8% Faculty & Staff
* 3% Parents

* **$4,159,995** Academic Programs

* **$829,544** Campus Events & Student Resource Centers
* **$337,435** Buildings & Facilities
* **$151,163** Athletics
* **$100,527** Bronco Fund Library

## Stories of Philanthropic Impact

![Stephanie and Al Tarkington](../img/tarkington,-al-and-steve2.jpg)

### Stephanie and Al Tarkington

[**Stephanie and Al Tarkington**](https://www.cpp.edu/cppmag/img/tarkington%2C-al-and-steve1.jpg) (’64, business administration) gave a gift of appreciated real estate to expand the [NASA-CPP Business Startup Program](https://www.cpp.edu/cppmag/market-ready.shtml), an innovation resource in the Center for Entrepreneurship and Innovation in the College of Business Administration. The gift increased a $1 million bequest intention.

![Earl Gales](../img/gales,-earl.jpg)

### Earl Gales Jr

[**Earl Gales Jr.**](planning-for-the-future.shtml) (’72, urban and regional planning) is founder and chairman of Jenkins/Gales & Martinez Inc., an architectural and construction management firm serving Southern California and international regions. He recently established an endowed scholarship in the College of Environmental Design, which will support the university’s commitment to diversity, inclusion and access.

![Jose Gomez](../img/gomez,-jose-dist-alum3.jpg)

### Jose Gomez

[**Jose Gomez**](bridging-the-gap.shtml) (’93, sociology), executive vice president and chief operating officer at Cal State LA, created the Gomez Family Basic Needs Endowment, which will help provide access to food, housing and crisis resources through the Bronco Cares Basic Needs Program.

![Suhani Bhakta](../img/20pres-council-scholar-bhakta7.jpg)

### Suhani Bhakta

[**Suhani Bhakta**](https://www.cpp.edu/president/honoring-exellence/bhakta.shtml), a graduate student in molecular biology and a Kim Family President’s Scholar, plans to earn a doctoral degree. “I’d like to encourage women, especially women of color, to pursue careers in STEM. When young girls witness intelligent and independent women conducting science, they will believe they can too,” she says.
