# Plan Your Path with Supportive, Personalized Advising

## CLASS Student Success Center

The CLASS Student Success Center can help you with the following:

* General Academic Advising
* Orientation, Registration, and Graduation Questions
* Course Petitions
* Degree Progress Reports (DPR) Review
* Transfer Credit Report (TCR) Review
* California Promise Advising
* CPP Connect Planner Review
* Academic Warnings, Academic Notices, and Academic Notices with Contracts
* Resource Referral

**NOTE: All incoming transfer students must see a CLASS Student Sucess Center advisor BEFORE consulting their assigned Music Department advisor.**

[Visit the CLASS Student Success Center Website](https://www.cpp.edu/studentsuccess/oss/academic-advising/university-policy-information/academic-standing.shtml)

## Music Department Advising

For Music Department Advising (course selection, senior project, career options, etc.) please consult your assigned Music Department Advisor.

* **You are assigned an academic advisor during Orientation**, and advisor lists are posted near the department office.
  + Please contact the department at mu@cpp.eduif you do not know who your assigned advisor might be.
* You may also consult with the faculty members who are designated as advisors for your specific program.
* Students who are in their first year as a music major (first-years, incoming transfers, or those current CPP students changing from another major) will have a hold placed on their registration each semester, to assist in decision making during their transition year.
  + This hold is removed once the student meets with their advisor.
* Students with special needs (transfer credit evaluation, petitions, change of emphasis or option, etc.) may schedule an appointment during any semester with their advisor for individual meetings.
* "At-risk" students are those who have a GPA below 2.2.
  + The university computing system automatically places a hold on their registration.
  + They must see the At-Risk Advisor Maria Anzaldo (email mlanzaldo@cpp.edu) for removal of the hold.
