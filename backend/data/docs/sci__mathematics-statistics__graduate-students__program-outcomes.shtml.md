# Program Outcomes

## Program Learning Outcomes

1. Graduates will be prepared to pursue emphasis-related careers, such as post-secondary teaching, industry, public agencies, or pursue an advanced degree.
2. Graduates will show an advanced technical competency in the discipline of pure mathematics, applied mathematics, statistics, or mathematics education.
3. Graduates will communicate and work effectively and with integrity in individual and group settings.
4. Graduates will be prepared to pursue emphasis-related careers, such as post-secondary teaching, industry, public agencies, or pursue an advanced degree.
5. Graduates will communicate and work effectively and with integrity in individual and group settings.

## Master's in Statistics and Applied Mathematics Student Learning Outcomes

1. Students will be able to utilize foundational theory and generate written proofs of results in statistics and applied mathematics.
2. Students will be able to build, apply, and analyze statistical, mathematical, and simulation models.
3. Students will be able to present and defend mathematical projects orally.
4. Students will be able to identify and articulate ethical implications related to simulations and statistical models.

## Master's in Mathematics Student Learning Outcomes

1. Students will analyze the common body of knowledge in mathematics by reading and criticizing mathematical proofs.
2. Students will demonstrate the ability to apply analytical and theoretical skills to model and solve problems in their emphasis.
3. Students will perform and defend high-level reasoning.
4. Students will critically evaluate work in their discipline.
5. Students will effectively interpret and justify technical ideas with written and oral skills.
