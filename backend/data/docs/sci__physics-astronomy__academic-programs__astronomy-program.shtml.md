# Astronomy Program

**Come learn about the Universe!**

The Cal Poly Pomona Astronomy program provides opportunities for students to expand their knowledge of how the universe works, from how stars and planets form, to how galaxies get their structure, to the ultimate questions of the structure and fate of the entire universe.

The Astronomy program at Cal Poly Pomona offers courses, opportunities to become involved in cutting-edge research, and a thriving community of like-minded students and faculty, all working together to explore!

To learn more about our program, explore the links below.

![Students grouped up in a circle, discussing](../img/academic-programs/lecture-tutorial.jpg)

## Academics

The CPP Astronomy program offers a [variety of courses](course-descriptions.shtml) to satisfy your curiosity to know more about the Universe, from GE courses for everyone, to upper division courses for physics majors and others who have taken the first two years of the physics sequence (including physics and astronomy minors).

## Cal-Bridge

[Cal-Bridge](https://www.cpp.edu/calbridge/index.shtml) is a joint CSU-UC program, which provides scholarships, mentoring, and professional development for physics majors strongly motivated to pursue a PhD in physics or astronomy. Cal-Bridge also offers a summer research program (formerly called CAMPARE) providing opportunities for CPP students to engage in meaningful research in astronomy, planetary science, and astrobiology at one of numerous research sites across the country.
