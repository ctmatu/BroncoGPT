# International Students

## Apply as an International Transfer

Requirements for Transfers who are from U.S. colleges/universities (If you have attended or are currently attending a college/university inside the U.S.)

### From U.S. College/University: Admission Requirements

Prospective International applicants from U.S. College or Universities can be view via our [Transfer Requirements](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/transfer/requirements.shtml) webpage.

Additional criteria may be required for [impacted majors](../transfer/impacted-majors.shtml).

### From U.S. College/University: English Proficiency Requirement

English language proficiency for international transfer applicants from non-U.S. college/universities

| Exam | Undergraduate | Graduate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Test of English as a Foreign Language ([TOEFL](https://www.ets.org/toefl))  TOEFL iBT Home Edition and MyBest | 71 iBT  71 iBT | 80 iBT  80 iBT  100 (MA English Applicants) |
| International English Language Testing System ([IELTS](https://www.ielts.org/en-us)) | 6.0 | 6.5  7.0 (MA English Applicants) |
| [Duolingo](https://englishtest.duolingo.com/home)English Test | 95 | 105  120 (MA English Applicants) |

English Proficiency Waivers:

* If all transfer criteria (60 units and “Golden Four” with grade of C- or better) have been completed at a U.S. institution
* If some of your transfer criteria (60 units and “Golden Four” with grade of C- or better) have been completed at a foreign college or university, then you must take the TOEFL, IELTS or Duolingo exam and earn the minimum score indicated above. Please have your scores sent to Cal Poly Pomona directly from the test agency
* Countries where English is the national language include: Antigua, Australia, Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, Bermuda, Botswana, Canada (except Québec), Ghana, Guyana, Ireland, Jamaica, Kenya, New Zealand, Nigeria, Scotland, South Africa, Trinidad, the United Kingdom, the United States, Virgin Islands and Wales

### From U.S. College/University: Transcripts

After you submit your Cal State Apply application, you **may** be asked to provide CPP Office of Admission with your college transcripts. Only provide the transcripts (if requested) by the stated deadline in your applicant portal.

### Local Area Admissions

Applicants who apply to non-impacted majors and are from [local area schools](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/transfer/local-area.shtml) (Mt. Sac and Citrus Colleges) who meet the minimum CSU eligibility transfer requirements will be admitted. [Impacted majors](https://www.cpp.edu/admissions/transfer/impacted-majors.shtml) will be held to a higher admission eligibility and/or required additional pre-requisite courses.

## Requirements for Transfers who are from non-U.S. colleges/universities (If you have attended or are currently attending a college/university outside of the U.S.)

### From non-U.S. College/University: Admission Requirements

* 60 semester credits (approximately 2 years) from an accredited college/university
* At least 1 courses of College level Mathematics
* Good Academic Standing

Additional courses for [impacted majors](../transfer/impacted-majors.shtml) are strongly recommended (but not required).  Such courses will help demonstrate your preparation for the impacted major, and may be used for admission consideration.

### From non-U.S. College/University: English Proficiency Requirement

English language proficiency for international transfer applicants from U.S. college/universities

| Exam | Undergraduate | Graduate |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Test of English as a Foreign Language ([TOEFL](https://www.ets.org/toefl))  TOEFL iBT Home Edition and MyBest | 71 iBT  71 iBT | 80 iBT  80 iBT  100 (MA English Applicants) |
| International English Language Testing System ([IELTS](https://www.ielts.org/en-us)) | 6.0 | 6.5  7.0 (MA English Applicants) |
| [Duolingo](https://englishtest.duolingo.com/home)English Test | 95 | 105  120 (MA English Applicants) |

English Proficiency Waivers:

* If you have studied at least 3 years of full-time enrollment at the secondary level or above at an institution where English is the principal language of instruction.
* Countries where English is the national language include: Antigua, Australia, Bahamas, Barbados, Belize, Bermuda, Botswana, Canada (except Québec), Ghana, Guyana, Ireland, Jamaica, Kenya, New Zealand, Nigeria, Scotland, South Africa, Trinidad, the United Kingdom, the United States, Virgin Islands and Wales

### From non-U.S. College/University: Transcripts

After you submit your Cal State Apply application, you will be asked to provide CPP Office of Admission with your college transcripts.  The Office of Admissions will convert your international grades to the equivalent U.S. 4.0 grade scale in order to ascertain your academic profile.  When requested, you must provide:

* All transcripts/marksheets for college/university work completed
* English translation (if marksheets are not in English)
* If admitted to the university, you will need to provide OFFICIAL transcripts along with certified translations
