# Refund Policy

## Overview

Students who withdraw from all classes may be eligible for a tuition and fee refund. Refund eligibility and amount depend on the date of withdrawal and are governed by CSU policy and Title 5 of the California Code of Regulations.

Below you’ll find refund schedules and examples organized by term. Refunds may be prorated based on withdrawal date. All non-Financial Aid related refunds are subject to a $5.00 administrative fee.

## Additional Notes

* A $5 Refund Processing Fee applies to all out of pocket payment refunds.
* All refunds are issued to the student (exceptions include 529 payments and Parent Plus Loans)
* Housing and dining refunds follow separate policies—please contact University Housing or Dining Services.
* Students receiving financial aid should **contact the Financial Aid Office before withdrawing**, as this may impact aid eligibility or repayment.

**Fee Adjustments During Add/Drop Period (Spring 2026: January 5, 2026 to January 30, 2026)**

Students who adjust their enrollment during the add/drop period may be subject to a change in their tuition and fees. See above Refund Policies by Term.

Eligibility Criteria
To be eligible for the fee adjustment from full-time charges to part-time charges:

* The enrollment change must occur between the official add/drop period (January 5, 2026 to January 30, 2026).
* The new number of enrolled units must place the student in the 0-6 units part-time category *(Example: **If you were enrolled in 7.0 or more units and drop to 6.0 or fewer units during the add/drop period, you may be eligible for a fee adjustment.**)*
* The student must not be receiving a flat-rate tuition or special program pricing that restricts adjustments *(Example: participation in the WUE program).*

What’s Adjusted?

* Only the CSU Tuition Fee is eligible for adjustment.
* Campus-based fees will not be reduced, even if your unit load changes.

## CSU Fee Policy

The CSU makes every effort to keep student costs to a minimum. Tuition and fees listed in published schedules or student accounts may need to be increased when public funding is inadequate. Therefore, CSU reserves the right, even after tuition or fees are initially charged or initial payments are made, to increase or modify any listed tuition or fees. All listed fees, other than mandatory systemwide tuition, are subject to change without notice, until the date when instruction for a particular semester or quarter has begun. All CSU-listed tuition and fees should be regarded as estimates that are subject to change upon approval by the Board of Trustees, the chancellor or the presidents, as appropriate. Changes in mandatory systemwide tuition will be made in accordance with the requirements of the Working Families Student Fee Transparency and Accountability Act (Sections 66028 through 66028.6 of the California Education)
