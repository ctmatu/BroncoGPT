# Past Fellows

## FELLOWSHIP THROUGH THE YEARS

We honor the faculty and student fellows whose past contributions have helped shape the work and mission of the Digital Humanities Consortium. Their commitment to interdisciplinary collaboration and innovation continues to inspire our community.

## 2004-2005 Faculty Fellows

[![Lauren Furey](img/laurenfurey.png)

### Associate Professor of Communication

#### Lauren Furey

She advises The Poly Post student newspaper and Bronco Guide magazine, as well as co-leads a Media and Politics Research Team with undergraduate students. Outside of teaching, she researches the portrayal of women in politics by the news media and in popular culture and co-hosts a podcast featuring interviews with women politicians called The Pink State, which won an award of excellence at the 2024 Broadcast Education Association’s Media Arts Festival.](https://open.spotify.com/show/02KE9vSfDrnGLonB1kJV0l%20 "opens a new window")

[![Robert Lewis](img/lewis.jpg)

### Associate Professor of History

#### Robert Lewis

Specializing in modern European history, with a focus on France, urban history, and the history of sport. He is the author of The Stadium Century: Sport, Spectatorship and Society in Modern France. His new research digitizes war-related materials, and he supervises students’ DH projects oriented around the global history of the First World War.](https://www.cpp.edu/faculty/rwlewis/index.shtml%20 "opens a new window")

[![Denisse Vasquez-Guevara ](img/denise.jpg)

### Assistant Professor of Communication

#### Denisse Vasquez-Guevara

As a science and health communication researcher, she has worked in multidisciplinary research on child nutrition, COVID-19 prevention, chronic illness management, urban accessibility, and sustainable foods, creating educational resources via online platforms. She was included in the Ecuadorean Women in Science Hall by REMCI and awarded as Top Faculty Instructor at the University of Cuenca’s School of Communication. At the DHC, through The Scicom Toolbox, she will provide public engagement tools and training through social media and in-person science communication for culturally diverse audiences.](https://www.youtube.com/%40ScicomToolbox-n6c%20 "opens a new window")

## 2004-2005 Student Fellows

![Katie Priest](img/katie.jpg)

### Katie Priest

Katie Priest is a Double Major in Philosophy and Communication and a writer and audio editor for The Poly Post’s podcasts. She was a 2023-2024 CCEP Ethics & Policy Student Fellow and winner of the Philosophy Department’s Pai award for Outstanding Service. She leads workshops on podcast editing for the DHC, including classroom visits.

![Pedro Rosales ](img/pedro.jpg)

### Pedro Rosales

He is a Sociology Major and a founding member and Video Editor for the Criminology Media Lab. His research for the lab was featured in the 15th Lowell Overton Undergraduate Research Symposium. He is also one of the lead student organizers for Bat Night. He leads workshops on video editing for the DHC, including classroom visits
