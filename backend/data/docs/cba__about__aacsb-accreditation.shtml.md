# AACSB Accreditation

## About AACSB Accreditation

![Graduate during commencement holding degree and celebrating](../img/aacsb-grad-no-decorations.jpg)The undergraduate programs of the College of Business Administration and the graduate degrees offered by the [Singelyn Graduate School of Business](https://www.cpp.edu/cba/graduate-business-programs/index.shtml) are accredited by the [Association to Advance Collegiate Schools of Business (AACSB)](https://www.aacsb.edu/). All our degree programs meet the rigorous requirements of AACSB accreditation focusing on the principles of continuous improvement, demonstrated student learning, societal impact and mission-aligned curricula.

AACSB accreditation is globally recognized as the longest-standing and most recognized form of business school accreditation. Accreditation is a voluntary, nongovernmental process that includes a rigorous external review of a school's mission, faculty qualifications, curricula and ability to provide the highest-quality programs while making a societal impact. AACSB is recognized by the world's top employers, allowing our students access to broad career opportunities.

AACSB accreditation is achieved by only 6% of business schools worldwide. Recent surveys demonstrate that graduates of AACSB-accredited business schools have high employability, degree satisfaction and career advancement rates. This accreditation allows our students to be part of the prestigious [Beta Gamma Sigma honor society](https://www.betagammasigma.org/home) and, in general, places us among the [best education programs in the world](https://www.aacsb.edu/learners/tools/why-accreditation-matters).

## Did you know?

### Rigorous Standards

Less than 6 percent of business schools worldwide have obtained AACSB accreditation. CBA is one of them!

### Desirable Graduates

AACSB graduates are sought by top, global organizations that only recruit from AACSB-accredited schools.

### Satisfied Employers

Recent surveys show that graduates of AACSB-accredited business schools have high employability, degree satisfaction and career advancement rates (according to AACSB, based on information collected independently by GMAC®, NASBA and AACSB International).
