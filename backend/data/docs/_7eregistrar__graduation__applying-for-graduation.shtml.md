# Apply for Graduation

## How to...

You may wish to view the [How to Apply for Graduation Online via BroncoDirect (PDF)](../forms/how-to-apply-for-graduation-online-via-broncodirect.pdf) form if you need a step-by-step guide on how to apply online. Students must submit a graduation application through [BroncoDirect](https://www.cpp.edu/broncodirect/) by the appropriate deadline. Students failing to submit their application by the appropriate deadline may be required to graduate in a future semester. Do not apply online if you do not see your desired graduation term in BroncoDirect.  Please email registrar@cpp.edu for assistance.

## Graduation Fee

After you apply for graduation, you will need to pay a non-refundable Graduation Application Fee. You only pay the fee once per academic career and program. You can pay in your BroncoDirect Student Center (view [How to Pay Your Graduation Fees on BroncoDirect (PDF))](../forms/how-to-pay-your-graduation-fees-on-broncodirect.pdf). Failing to pay the fee may prevent your diploma from being released.

* Application Fee through Summer 2025: $59
* Application Fee Beginning Fall 2025: $125

The fee covers the cost of your diploma,  administrative review, commencement ceremony, and more.  If you have questions about the Commencement ceremonies, email commencement@cpp.edu.

**When the Fee is Charged**

**Undergraduate Students:**
When your status changes on your Degree Progress Report to any of the following, you will receive an email and the fee charged on your Student Center account balance:

* Approved
* Degree Awarded
* Needs to Finish Pending Work
* Pending Administrative
* Program in Review

 The Registrar’s Office will begin reviewing applications March 1 for spring graduates, August 1 for summer graduates, and October 1 for fall graduates.

**Graduate Students:**

Your status on your Degree Progress Report (if applicable) will change to “Applied for Graduation” at the end of your graduation term.  You will receive an email and the fee charged on your Student Center account balance.

**Credential Only Students:**
Students completing a credential only should visit the **[College of Education and Integrative Studies](https://www.cpp.edu/ceis/commencement/ceis-commencement-credential-programs.shtml)** website for commencement information specific to credential completers.

## Additional Information:

### Apply for Graduation Eligibility

To be able to access the graduation application in your BroncoDirect Student Center, you must meet all of the following eligibility criteria:

 **Undergraduate**:

* **Earned Units:** 85+ earned units

* **GPA:** 1.85+ Cumulative GPA

* **Remaining Units:** No more than 36 (including in Progress units) units remaining toward degree completion

 If you meet these requirements but do not see the link, email the Bronco Advising Center at bac@cpp.edu.

**Graduate**:

* **Earned Units:**  9+ earned units
* **GPA:**  2.75+ Cumulative GPA

If you meet these requirements but do not see the link, email the Registrar’s Office at registrar@cpp.edu.

### Clearing for Graduation

* Once grades are posted, your record will be evaluated within 4-6 weeks and if all requirements are met, your degree will be awarded and your unofficial transcript will show your degree.
* Order an official transcript from the Registrar’s Office if you need to provide degree verification to an employer or another institution.

### Graduation Deferral Notification

* Notification about your unsatisfied graduation requirements, which includes failure to submit necessary documentation by the appropriate deadline, will be e-mailed to your Cal Poly Pomona email account.
* Your [Transfer & Graduation Specialist](https://www.cpp.edu/registrar/graduation/transfer-and-graduation-advisors.shtml) will indicate on this email that your graduation has been deferred.
* Deferred candidates must reapply for graduation to be considered for graduation again. Submit a new Graduation Application online.

### Graduation Deficiencies Notification

* Lack of approved document(s) is considered as a graduation deficiency.
* Notification regarding your graduation deficiencies will be sent to your Cal Poly Pomona email account.

### Graduation "Out of Residency"

Graduation “out of residency” means when a student is discontinued from the university but may have fulfilled or is very close to fulfilling their final requirements (within 112 units) after leaving the institution.  In consultation with an academic advisor, the student may be considered for graduation without reenrolling in the university if the advisor can confirm that the student has completed all the remaining requirements through courses or via substitution petitions.  Once determined, they may petition to graduate while remaining in discontinued status.

Students must meet the following criteria:

* earned at least 112 units toward the degree requirement
* in good academic standing

If the remaining degree requirements are unfulfilled, the student may reapply for admission or complete the outstanding courses through the College of Professional and Global Education (CPGE). Students will need to pay the graduation application fee and may be eligible to participate in commencement if they have not done so in the past.  For detailed information, contact the Registrar’s Office at registrar@cpp.edu.

### Commencement Ceremonies

* Any student who receives a Cal Poly Pomona degree during the academic year (Fall, Spring and Summer) is automatically eligible to participate in that year’s Commencement Ceremony held in May.
* Information regarding the Commencement Ceremony may be obtained at [Commencement website](https://www.cpp.edu/~commencement/) or from your major department.
