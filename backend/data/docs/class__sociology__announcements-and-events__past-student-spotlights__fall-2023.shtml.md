# Department Events, Fall 2023

## Department of Sociology Welcomes Hollencrest Middle School Students

![](../events/flyers/middle-school-visit1.jpg)

Hollencrest Middle School 8th Students begin their visit at Cal Poly Pomona... exploring CPP's Japanese garden, Rose Garden, ponds, architecture, and Rain Bird Bio Trek before joining a lecture...

Sixteen Students from the AVID Program at Hollencrest Middle School visited the Department of Sociology on 9/28/23. The schedule included:

* A campus walking tour.
* A lecture.
* Two sessions with faculty and CPP students about our programs in the Sociology Department.

In small groups, 8th graders had an opportunity to ask questions. After these informal sessions, a visit to the BRIC and seeing the climbing wall was on the agenda.

![](../events/flyers/middle-school-visit2.jpg)

Middle School Students attend **Dr. Fong**'s Contemporary Sociology Class.

![](../events/flyers/img_0096.jpg)

The Lecture was titled *Herbert Marcuse's notion of one dimensionality.***Dr. Fong** exemplifies Marcuse's concept of one dimensionality through examples of mass media and mass culture, displaying various advertisements.

![](../events/flyers/middle-schoool-crm-club2.jpg)

*(Left to right back row) **Javier Gomez Meza, Vivian Martinez, Jasmine Limas, Ryan Chen**;**(Front) **Luna Manriquez-Moran** (Vice President) & **Alexis Rivera** (President).*

The 8th graders had a chance to speak with members of the Criminology Club about CPP and their student experiences.

![](../events/flyers/middle-school-visit5.jpg)

**Alexis Rivera** and **Ryan Chen** respond to 8th graders inquiries about courses and careers in Criminology.

![](../events/flyers/middle-school-visit4.jpg)

**Jasmine Penate**, a Peer Mentor and an e-board member of the AKD Sociology Honors Society, talks to 8th graders about research - what she enjoys about research and what she would like to do after graduating from Cal Poly. Her McNair research project examines *Factors that contribute to a shift away from Indigenous Identities and Cultures to Mainstream Western Culture Among Indigenous Guatemalans.*
