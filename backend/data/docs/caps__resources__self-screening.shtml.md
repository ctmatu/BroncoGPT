# Mental Health Self-Screening

## About Mental Health Self-Screening

Mental Health America (MHA) is the nation's leading community-based nonprofit organization dedicated to addressing the needs of those living with mental illness and promoting overall mental health for everyone.  [Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/)'s work is focused on prevention services for all; early identification and intervention for those at risk; integrated care, services, and support for those who need it; and recovery as a goal.  [Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/) provides scientifically validated online mental health screenings that are free, confidential, and anonymous to help individuals understand and learn about their current mental health. For more information, visit [Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/).

**Counseling and Psychological Services** is providing this resource for the campus community for the purpose of the community gaining awareness of their own mental health.  Accessing these self-screening tools will take you to an external site managed by [Mental Health America](https://mhanational.org/) and are accessible at user's discretion. **Counseling and Psychological Services** does not monitor nor receive information or data regarding the results of any tools.  No response based on your results will be made by **Counseling and Psychological Services**. If you would like to discuss your results with a mental health professional, [contact or visit us](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/about/contacting-caps.shtml) to make an appointment.

Students who have thoughts of suicide, or harming oneself or others, are strongly encouraged to [contact or visit us](https://www.cpp.edu/caps/about/contacting-caps.shtml).

For additional resources, visit the [Jed Foundation's Mental Health Resources Center](https://jedfoundation.org/mental-health-resource-center/).
